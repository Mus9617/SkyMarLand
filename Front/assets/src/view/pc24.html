The Pliatus PC-24

The Pilatus PC-24 is a light business jet produced by Pilatus Aircraft of Switzerland. The aircraft was introduced in 2015 and is the first jet aircraft developed by Pilatus, best known for the PC-12 single-engine turboprop business aircraft. The PC-24 is a twin-engine jet with a supercritical wing, designed to take off from short and unprepared runways. The aircraft is powered by two Williams FJ44 turbofan engines and has a range of 2,000 nautical miles (3,700 km). The PC-24 can carry up to 10 passengers and has a maximum cruise speed of 440 knots (810 km/h). The aircraft is certified for single-pilot operation and has a full glass cockpit with touchscreen avionics. The PC-24 is designed for corporate, charter, and utility missions, and is also used by air ambulance operators and government agencies. The aircraft has a large cargo door for easy loading and unloading of passengers and cargo. The PC-24 is known for its versatility, performance, and reliability, and has been well received by customers around the world.
