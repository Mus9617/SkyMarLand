Cessna Citation CJ4

The Cessna Citation CJ4 is a light business jet produced by Cessna, a subsidiary of Textron Aviation. The aircraft was introduced in 2010 and is the fourth model in the CitationJet/CJ series, which includes the CJ1, CJ2, and CJ3. The CJ4 is a twin-engine jet with a T-tail and swept wing, designed for short to medium-range flights. The aircraft is powered by two Williams FJ44 turbofan engines and has a range of 2,165 nautical miles (4,010 km). The CJ4 can carry up to 10 passengers and has a maximum cruise speed of 451 knots (835 km/h). The aircraft is certified for single-pilot operation and has a full glass cockpit with touchscreen avionics. The CJ4 is designed for corporate, charter, and utility missions, and is also used by air ambulance operators and government agencies. The aircraft has a large cargo door for easy loading and unloading of passengers and cargo. The CJ4 is known for its versatility, performance, and reliability, and has been well received by customers around the world.
