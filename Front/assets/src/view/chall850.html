Challenger 850

The Bombardier Challenger 850 is a large business jet produced by Bombardier Aerospace. The aircraft was introduced in 2006 and is based on the Bombardier CRJ200 regional jet. The Challenger 850 is a twin-engine jet with a T-tail and swept wing, designed for long-range flights. The aircraft is powered by two General Electric CF34 turbofan engines and has a range of 2,811 nautical miles (5,200 km). The Challenger 850 can carry up to 15 passengers and has a maximum cruise speed of 459 knots (850 km/h). The aircraft is certified for single-pilot operation and has a full glass cockpit with touchscreen avionics. The Challenger 850 is designed for corporate, charter, and utility missions, and is also used by air ambulance operators and government agencies. The aircraft has a large cargo door for easy loading and unloading of passengers and cargo. The Challenger 850 is known for its versatility, performance, and reliability, and has been well received by customers around the world.
